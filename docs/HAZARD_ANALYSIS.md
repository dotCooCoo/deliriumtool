# Hazard Analysis — Delirium Bedside Reference Tool (deliriumtool.com)

**Document type:** Internal design/QA artifact. Risk analysis in the *spirit* of ISO 14971 (risk management) paired with an IEC 62366 use-error pass. This is **not** a regulatory submission and does not assert device status; the tool is an internal, non-distributed, client-side **reference aid** that is outside FDA premarket reach. The rubric here is voluntary: hold each output to the standard that it *would* qualify as **Non-Device CDS** under the 21st Century Cures Act §520(o)(1)(E) (Jan-2026 CDS final-guidance 4-criteria structure) — in particular criterion (3) *supports rather than directs* and criterion (4) *the clinician can independently review the basis*. Anywhere the tool emits a **number that drives an action**, that number implies a calibration the "explicitly unvalidated" framing disclaims, and is treated as a hazard source.

**Scope of analysis:** the on-screen tool (`src/index.html`), the scoring logic (`src/js/scoring.js`), the medication data (`src/js/data/meds.js`), the citation registry (`src/js/data/refs.js`), the exported PDFs (`src/js/pdf.js`), and the share/save model (`src/js/share.js`, localStorage autosave, JSON export). This is framework, not legal advice.

## Method and rating key

Each hazard is characterised by the harm it could cause, the existing in-tool mitigation, a **residual-risk rating after that mitigation**, and a recommended additional control. Residual risk is a qualitative product of **severity** (negligible → catastrophic) and **likelihood of the use error / misread occurring in normal bedside use**:

- **Low** — would require an atypical chain of misuse; existing mitigation is adequate.
- **Moderate** — a foreseeable single misread/misuse reaches a clinically meaningful (reversible) harm, or mitigation is present on one surface but not on the surface that travels (e.g., screen but not PDF).
- **High** — a foreseeable single misread reaches a serious/irreversible harm, or the output itself is miscalibrated against its cited basis.

A recurring structural finding drives several ratings: **the exported PDF is the artifact that enters the chart, handoff, and printout, but the PDF carries only the generic footer "Reference aid only — follow local policy & prescriber/pharmacy review."** The richer on-screen caveats ("not a validated score," "heuristic," "proportion not prevalence," "screen not diagnosis," renal/hepatic cautions) do **not** travel onto the PDF. Mitigations that live only on screen are therefore discounted for any harm that materialises off-screen.

## IEC 62366 use-error focus (summary)

The use-errors with the clearest harm path are: (1) reading `N/17 (tier)` on the **printout** as a probability/diagnosis (H1) because the qualifying context is screen-only; (2) the **5-card ASE** over-calling inattention in the very patients routed to it (H5 — the one genuine calibration defect); (3) **confusing the safe "Share setup" channel with the PHI-bearing Save/Export/PDF channels** on a shared workstation (H12); and (4) **acting on the all-on 104-agent list** by stopping a needed drug, or ignoring it entirely (H9). These four should be prioritised; H5 (correctness) and H1/H12 (caveat-and-channel travel) are the highest-leverage fixes.

## Residual-risk posture

After the recommended controls, the design intent is that **every action-driving number is either (a) faithful to a validated, cited instrument (CAM-ICU, RASS, PADIS-anchored dosing) or (b) explicitly flattened/relabelled as "pragmatic, not validated"** — and that the caveat travels onto whichever surface (screen, PDF, share, export) the number travels on. That posture keeps each output buildable to satisfy Non-Device CDS criteria (3) *supports rather than directs* and (4) *basis independently reviewable*.
