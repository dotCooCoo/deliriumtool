# Hazard Analysis — Delirium Bedside Reference Tool (deliriumtool.com)

**Document type:** Internal design/QA artifact. Risk analysis in the *spirit* of ISO 14971 (risk management) paired with an IEC 62366 use-error pass. This is **not** a regulatory submission and does not assert device status; the tool is an internal, non-distributed, client-side **reference aid** that is outside FDA premarket reach. The rubric here is voluntary: hold each output to the standard that it *would* qualify as **Non-Device CDS** under the 21st Century Cures Act §520(o)(1)(E) (Jan-2026 CDS final-guidance 4-criteria structure) — in particular criterion (3) *supports rather than directs* and criterion (4) *the clinician can independently review the basis*. Anywhere the tool emits a **number that drives an action**, that number implies a calibration the "explicitly unvalidated" framing disclaims, and is treated as a hazard source.

**Scope of analysis:** the on-screen tools (`src/index.html`, `src/peds/index.html`, `src/templates/index.html`), the scoring logic (`src/js/scoring.js`, `src/js/peds/scoring.js`), the medication data (`src/js/data/meds.js`, `src/js/peds/data/meds.js`), the citation registries, the exported PDFs (`src/js/pdf.js`, `src/js/peds/report.js`, `src/js/templates/pdf.js`), and the share/save model (`src/js/share.js`, localStorage autosave, JSON export). This is framework, not legal advice.

Earlier revisions of this analysis identified hazards that have since been remediated (weighted risk scores, a miscalibrated 5-card Pictures-ASE cut-point, a score-triggered psychiatry consult, a mislabeled deep-sedation preset, an uncited thiamine dose, the superseded CAM-ICU Feature-3 wording, and a bypassable arousal gate). Each resolution is dated in `CLINICAL_METHODOLOGY.md` §6.5; the rows below describe the **current** state and the residual risk that remains after those fixes.

## Method and rating key

Each hazard is characterised by the harm it could cause, the existing in-tool mitigation, a **residual-risk rating after that mitigation**, and a recommended additional control. Residual risk is a qualitative product of **severity** (negligible → catastrophic) and **likelihood of the use error / misread occurring in normal bedside use**:

- **Low** — would require an atypical chain of misuse; existing mitigation is adequate.
- **Moderate** — a foreseeable single misread/misuse reaches a clinically meaningful (reversible) harm, or mitigation is present on one surface but not on the surface that travels (e.g., screen but not PDF).
- **High** — a foreseeable single misread reaches a serious/irreversible harm, or the output itself is miscalibrated against its cited basis.

A structural principle drives several ratings: **the exported PDF is the artifact that enters the chart, handoff, and printout.** A mitigation that lives only on screen is discounted for any harm that materialises off-screen — which is why the 2026-07-01 revision aligned the printed SPA/rounding surfaces (haloperidol IV caution, dose-free acetaminophen, adult-ICU scope, subtype denominator) with the on-screen wording.

## IEC 62366 use-error focus (summary)

With the earlier correctness defects resolved (Pictures-ASE calibration, Feature-3 wording, the bypassable arousal gate — see §6.5 of the methodology), the use-errors with the clearest remaining harm paths are: (1) reading the printed risk count as a probability (H1); (2) dosing an antipsychotic without the pre-dose QT/electrolyte check the tool describes but does not yet co-locate as a checklist (H2); and (3) **confusing the safe "Share setup" channel with the PHI-bearing Save/Export/PDF channels** on a shared workstation (H10). These are the highest-leverage open mitigations.

## Residual-risk posture

After the recommended controls, the design intent is that **every action-driving number is either (a) faithful to a validated, cited instrument (CAM-ICU, RASS, CAPD, pCAM-ICU, psCAM-ICU, SBS, PADIS-anchored dosing) or (b) explicitly flattened/relabelled as "pragmatic, not validated"** — and that the caveat travels onto whichever surface (screen, PDF, template sheet, share, export) the number travels on. That posture keeps each output buildable to satisfy Non-Device CDS criteria (3) *supports rather than directs* and (4) *basis independently reviewable*.
