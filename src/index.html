Built for the adult ICU. CAM-ICU, RASS, and the ABCDEF bundle are validated for adult intensive-care patients (ICDSC is the in-ICU alternative screen) — not for other settings. Elsewhere, use the setting-appropriate validated tool:
  • Emergency department → bCAM (DTS → bCAM) or 4AT
  • General / step-down ward → 3D-CAM or 4AT
  • Pediatric ICU → pCAM-ICU (≥ 5 yr), psCAM-ICU (6 mo–5 yr), or CAPD (all ages)
  • Pregnancy → the adult ICU screens still apply, but adjust medication safety and dosing

    CAM-ICU Screening
    Perform once per shift and with any acute change in mental status
    1Acute onset & fluctuating course Required

    Evidence of acute change from mental status baseline, OR fluctuation in mental status in the past 24 hours (e.g., on RASS/GCS or a previous delirium assessment)? Anchor "baseline" with family/collateral on admission — Feature 1 cannot be scored without it.

    2Inattention Required

    Letters task: say S-A-V-E-A-H-A-A-R-T (10 letters, ~1 every 3 sec); patient squeezes on each "A".

    Can't do letters (deaf, language barrier, non-verbal)? Use the Pictures ASE — show 5 pictures to memorize, then 10 recognition pictures (yes/no); >2 errors out of 10 = positive (same 0–10 scale as the letters task).

    3Altered level of consciousness

    Feature 3 is read from the RASS you documented above — present whenever the RASS is anything other than alert and calm (zero), so there is nothing to enter here.

    4Disorganized thinking

    Ask the 4 yes/no questions, then the command. Disorganized thinking present if combined errors >1.

    1. Will a stone float on water?
    2. Are there fish in the sea?
    3. Does one pound weigh more than two pounds?
    4. Can you use a hammer to pound a nail?

    Command: "Hold up this many fingers" (examiner holds up 2), then "Now do the same thing with the other hand" — do not repeat the number of fingers. If the patient cannot move both arms, for the second part ask them to "Add one more finger". Any wrong answer or failure to complete the entire command counts as one error.

    Delirium status
    Incomplete
    Complete Features 1 & 2 + one of 3 or 4
    F1 Acute/fluctuating F2 Inattention 3 or 4 LOC / thinking
    Positive if 1 AND 2 AND (3 OR 4)

    Limitations: score against the patient's baseline; reduced accuracy in primary neurologic injury/TBI, aphasia, or deep sedation. Missed cases are predominantly hypoactive — screen every shift.

    A positive CAM-ICU is a screen, not a diagnosis — confirm clinically and exclude mimics.

    Delirium vs mimics
    Dementia — chronic, stable; delirium is acute, fluctuating, with inattention.
    Depression — can look hypoactive; mood-driven, attention less affected.
    Receptive aphasia / focal stroke — focal deficit, not global inattention → neuro exam + imaging.
    Non-convulsive seizures / status — unexplained ↓LOC → EEG.
    Catatonia — posturing, mutism, waxy flexibility.
    Intoxication / withdrawal — substance history; treat the cause.
    These can coexist with delirium — known dementia does not exclude superimposed delirium.

      SAT / SBT safety screen & failure criteria

      SAT — do NOT interrupt sedation if: active seizures · alcohol withdrawal on sedation · escalating sedation for agitation · neuromuscular blockade · MI in prior 24 h · raised ICP.

      SAT failure → stop SAT, restart sedation at HALF the prior dose if: sustained anxiety/agitation/pain · RR >35 for >5 min · SpO₂ <88% for >5 min · acute dysrhythmia · ≥2 signs of respiratory distress.

      SBT — proceed only if: SpO₂ ≥88% on FiO₂ ≤50% & PEEP ≤7.5 · no agitation · no ischemia in 24 h · hemodynamically stable · no raised ICP.

      SBT failure → abort, resume support if: RR >35 or <8 · SpO₂ <88% · abrupt mental-status change · dysrhythmia · ≥2 distress signs.

      Loop: pass SAT → proceed to SBT; fail SAT → restart sedation at half the prior dose; fail SBT → resume full ventilatory support (restart sedation at half the prior dose if needed); re-screen in 24 h.

      C — Choice of Analgesia & Sedation

      eCASH: minimal sedation is the default — analgesia first, lightest RASS that meets the goal, reassess each shift.

      D — Delirium: Assess, Prevent & Manage
      E — Early Mobility & Exercise

      Safety screen first — reddest parameter wins; a single red = hold & discuss with the team.

      ● Respiratory: FiO₂ <0.6 · SpO₂ >90% · RR <30
      ● Cardiovascular: stable BP, no escalating vasopressors, no active ischemia/arrhythmia
      ● Neuro: RASS-arousable & able to participate
      ● Lines/other: no unstable airway, active bleeding, or unstable fracture

      Progression: passive ROM → active ROM / bed exercises → sit / edge of bed → stand / transfer → ambulate.

        Fever, leukocytosis, cultures pending? Occult sepsis?

        Urinary retention or constipation? Bladder scan; disimpact or catheterize if indicated.

        I
        Ictal / Seizure

        Consider non-convulsive seizures / status — esp. with unexplained ↓LOC. Consider EEG.

        Under-hydration / Nutrition

        Volume status, intake, electrolytes. Parenteral thiamine before glucose. At-risk (malnutrition / alcohol use): 100–300 mg IV daily. Suspected Wernicke — guidelines diverge (low-certainty evidence): EFNS 200 mg IV TID or RCP 500 mg IV TID × 2–3 days then 250 mg taper.

        Na, Mg, Ca, glucose, BUN/Cr, acid-base, liver function. Pain/retention/constipation?

        Subdural / Sleep

        Subdural hematoma (recent fall or anticoagulation)? Sleep deprivation?

            Medications Reference
            Pharmacologic options and deliriogenic agents to review & limit
            Reminder: No agent has FDA approval for delirium treatment. Antipsychotics do not treat or shorten delirium (MIND-USA negative; PADIS 2025: unable to recommend for or against) — they control dangerous agitation short-term, then stop. Medications address symptoms only when non-pharmacologic measures fail and safety is at risk.
            Pharmacologic Options — Safety Indication Only
            Drug Typical Dose Notes
            Haloperidol 0.25–0.5 mg q4–6h PRN; lowest effective dose, cap per local protocol Elderly more sensitive (EPS/QTc rise with dose); ECG baseline; monitor QTc (caution >500 ms); avoid Parkinson's / Lewy body; dementia-related-mortality boxed warning; IV route is off-label (higher QT/Torsades risk) — prefer PO/IM, continuous ECG if given IV. Stop & escalate if NMS (rigidity + hyperthermia + autonomic instability + ↑CK).
            Quetiapine 12.5–25 mg q12h PO; lowest dose, shortest duration Sedating; orthostatic hypotension (fall risk); QTc prolongation; antipsychotic-class boxed warning — increased mortality in elderly with dementia-related psychosis.
            Dexmedetomidine 0.2–0.7 mcg/kg/hr IV infusion (label maintenance range) MV adults: agitation precluding weaning/extubation (PADIS 2018); or where light sedation / delirium reduction is the highest priority — suggested over propofol (PADIS 2025). A2B RCT: no extubation benefit vs propofol, more agitation and severe bradycardia — reserve the preference for when delirium reduction / light sedation is the priority. Monitor bradycardia/hypotension
            Lorazepam Specific Use Per CIWA protocol Rescue / alcohol withdrawal ONLY — may worsen delirium
            Melatonin 0.5–3 mg nightly (conventional sleep dosing — trial doses were 3–5 mg; Pro-MEDIC used 4 mg) PADIS 2025 conditionally suggests melatonin in adult ICU patients (low certainty; may reduce delirium prevalence and improve perceived sleep — no specific dose recommended); the largest RCT (Pro-MEDIC) was negative; not a treatment for established delirium. Ramelteon is the FDA-regulated melatonin-receptor agonist PADIS 2025 names as an alternative.

            These are conventional / expert starting references for short-term agitation control — not RCT- or guideline-calibrated delirium doses. Dexmedetomidine is a sedative infusion for ventilated patients, not a PRN antipsychotic-equivalent.

            No antipsychotics at discharge without psychiatric indication.
            Deliriogenic Medications — Review & Limit
            This is a medication-review prompt, not a list of equally harmful drugs. Risk varies by agent, dose, route, renal/hepatic function, drug interactions, and temporal association with the delirium. Some agents (e.g., benzodiazepines, strong anticholinergics, meperidine) are higher-risk; others warrant review only in specific contexts. Beers Criteria apply to adults ≥65.
            Renal/hepatic accumulation: meperidine — avoid; higher neurotoxicity risk than other opioids (normeperidine); morphine / hydromorphone glucuronide metabolites accumulate in renal impairment with neuroexcitatory toxicity; gabapentin / pregabalin — reduce dose at CrCl <60 (CNS adverse effects).
            Toggle any medication off to remove it from the printed documents. Changes apply to both the Full Rounding Tool and the SPA Quick Reference.
